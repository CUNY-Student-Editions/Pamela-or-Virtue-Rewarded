the common Translation: i.e. the metrical translation of Thomas Sternhold and John Hopkins (first fully published in 1562 and known as the Old Version), from which all the psalms in this passage are quoted. Though losing ground to the New Version of Nahum Tate and Nicholas Brady (1696), it was still, fifty years after Pamela, ‘used in more than half the parish churches in the kingdom’ (Edward Miller, Thoughts on the Present Performance of Psalmody (1791), 27). On Richardson’s use of this source, see M. Austin, ‘Lincolnshire Babylon: Competing Typologies in Pamela’s 137th Psalm’, Eighteenth-Century Fiction, 12 (2000), 501–14.
