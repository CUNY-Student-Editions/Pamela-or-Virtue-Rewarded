dear Quadrille: ‘began to take the place of ombre as the fashionable card game about 1726, and was in turn superseded by whist’ (OED2). For the reputation of the game as an idle or suspect pastime, see Gentleman’s Magazine, 6 (July and Sept. 1736), 373–4 and 514; also Clarissa, 538, where Lovelace plays quadrille with Sinclair’s whores.
