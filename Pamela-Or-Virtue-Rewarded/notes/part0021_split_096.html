a Court Game … the prime Minister: on card-games as a medium of political innuendo in the period, see H. Erskine-Hill, Poetry of Opposition and Revolution (1996), 77–83. Here the specific target is Sir Robert Walpole (1676–1745), ‘prime minister’ from 1721 to 1742, to whom the term (as denoting an office unconstitutionally fashioned by Walpole himself) had derogatory application (see e.g. Fielding, Champion, 8 May 1740).
