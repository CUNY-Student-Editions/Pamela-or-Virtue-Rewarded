such an Herod: Herod the Great, uxoricidal King of Judaea. ‘Herod directed, that his Mariamne should be put to death, that she might not be the wife of any other man, if he returned not alive from the court of Augustus Caesar’ (Grandison, ii. 619; cf. Clarissa, 749). SR probably had this story from Sir Roger L’Estrange’s translation of Josephus, ‘a half-sacred and half-profane author’ (Clarissa, 611): see Antiquities of the Jews, bk. xv, chs. iv and xi, in The Works of Flavius Josephus, 2nd edn. (1709). However, the story had been given wider currency by Addison in the Spectator (no. 171, 15 Sept. 1711), and Elijah Fenton’s play on the theme, Mariamne (1723), had been performed three times at Covent Garden in 1739.
