The Gentleman’s Advice: the manuscript version of Hill’s letter identifies the adviser as ‘that hearty good Friend of yours, Docr. Slocock’ (FM XIII, 2, fo. 36), i.e. Benjamin Slocock (1691–1753), who had recommended Pamela from the pulpit of St Saviour’s, Southwark, and to whom, in April 1741, Pamela Censured was ironically dedicated (E & K, 121, 123–4, 129; P. Sabor, ‘Did Richardson Bribe Dr. Slocock?’, Notes & Queries NS 26 (1979), 29–31).
