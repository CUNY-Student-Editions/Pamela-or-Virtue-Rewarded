EXPLANATORY NOTES

In order to minimize textual clutter and interruptions to reading, annotation is mainly confined to matters of historical, literary, or scriptural reference, and to the necessary explanation of customs and laws. A few glosses are included where particular comment or contextualization is called for, but in general readers puzzled by archaic, slang, or dialect usages should consult the Glossary below, where unfamiliar words and phrases are defined, together with the unusual or obsolete senses in which familiar words sometimes occur. Pamela’s narrative is heavily inflected by proverbial wisdom, both biblical and popular, and we have annotated only the most pointed examples: readers are referred to Peter Sabor’s Penguin edition of Pamela’s 1801 text for further illustration of the novel’s absorption of English folk proverbs.
