sensible Reader, reader of sensibility, capable of refined and tender feeling. For the same key contemporary sense of the word, see below, p. 7 (‘lamenting, with a most sensible Concern, the Miscarriages of her Hopes’); also Smollett, Peregrine Pickle (1751), ch. cxiv: ‘I shall leave the sensible reader to judge what passed … within the bosoms of the new-married couple.’
