VIRTUE Rewarded: by no means the first titular use of this phrase in prose fiction: cf. the anonymous novel Vertue Rewarded; or, The Irish Princess (1693), ed. H. McDermott (1992), and ‘Constant and Fidelia: or Virtue Rewarded’, a three-part serial tale in the Weekly Register (18 Sept. 2 Oct. 1731). For the similarities, see respectively P. Salzman, ‘Vertue Rewarded and Pamela’, Notes & Queries, NS 26 (1979), 554–6; M. Golden, ‘Public Context and Imagining Self in Pamela and Shamela’, ELH 53 (1986), 311–29 (at pp. 311–12).
