Birth-day Suit: rich dress worn at Court on royal birthdays, i.e. 30 October during the reign (1727–60) of George II, and 1 March until the death in 1737 of Queen Caroline (but see above, Introduction, pp. xiv–xv, on the novel’s ambiguous time-scheme). The most competitive displays were on the Queen’s birthday, when ‘the Nobility, &c. were dressed in an exceeding rich and grand Manner. The Ladies chiefly in Stuffs of Gold and Silver. The Gentlemen in cut and flower’d Velvets’ (Gentleman’s Magazine, 5 (Mar. 1735), 161).
