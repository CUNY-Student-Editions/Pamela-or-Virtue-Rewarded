Argus: Argus Panoptes, the all-seeing giant ordered by Juno to guard Io from Zeus, whose hundred eyes were metamorphosed into the peacock’s tail following his death at Mercury’s hands. ‘The head of Argus (as with Stars the Skies) | Was compass’d round, and wore an hundred eyes. | But two by turns their lids in slumber steep; | The rest on duty still their station keep; | Nor cou’d the total Constellation sleep’ (Dryden, ‘The First Book of Ovid’s Metamorphoses’ (1693), 11. 858–62).
