Nought can restrain Consent of Twain: cf. an erotic episode in Sir John Harington’s translation (1591) of Ariosto’s Orlando furioso, in which Fiametta, an innkeeper’s daughter, ingeniously contrives to make love to her low-born lover while in bed between two nobles. ‘In Fiametta that lying betweene a king and a knight tooke up a Tapster into her bed I can not tell what to note but that which Ovid saith: Non caret effectu, quod volvere duo. | Nought can restraine, consent of twaine’ (moral to bk. xxviii, quoting Ovid, Amores, 11. iii. 16). Lovelace recalls the same section from Ariosto in Clarissa, 912.
