Fool’s Plaything: a proverbial collocation, Williams being the fool here and Pamela his plaything or (in the paragraph that follows) bauble. Cf. William Taverner, ’Tis Well If It Takes (1719), 1. iii: ‘The World is very apt to slight every Thing for Fifteen [i.e. a girl of 15], and had rather play the Fool with a Bauble, than address Five and Twenty, and a good Understanding’; see also John Cleland, Memoirs of a Woman of Pleasure, ed. P. Sabor (1985), 162.
