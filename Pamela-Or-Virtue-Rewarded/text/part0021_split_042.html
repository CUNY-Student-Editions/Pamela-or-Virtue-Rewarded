Then, thought I … and no more?: a further possible echo of Sidney’s Philoclea, who locates the authority to end one’s own life with the unbounded will of God alone. ‘That we should be masters of ourselves, we can show at all no title, nor claim; since neither we made ourselves, nor bought ourselves, we can stand upon no other right but his gift, which he must limit as it pleaseth him’ (Old Arcadia, ed. Robertson, 297–8).
