And to lose the best Jewel… you propose to give me: Pamela echoes her parents’ first warning about ‘that Jewel, your Virtue, which no Riches … can make up to you’ (above, p. 14). The metaphor probably derives from Proverbs 31: 10, but was commonplace in the period: cf. Fielding, The Universal Gallant (1735), v. ii (‘Virtue is her greatest Jewel’).
