As Mr B. goes on to note (with sexually threatening innuendo), pressing was still used against felons who refused to plead in English courts. Sabor gives references for 1735 and 1741: in the earlier case, an accused murderer at the Lewes assizes was returned ‘to Horsham Gaol, and gradually press’d to Death, continuing obstinately Dumb to the last Moment’ (Gentleman’s Magazine, 5 (Aug. 1735), 497).
