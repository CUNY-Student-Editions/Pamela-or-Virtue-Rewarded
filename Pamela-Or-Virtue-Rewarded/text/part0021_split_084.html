Every one mend one: ‘… if all shall be mended (amended)’ (Oxford Dictionary of English Proverbs, 3rd edn. (1970), 230), a favourite saying of SR. Cf. Pamela’s continuation of 1741, iii. 282 (‘Give Praise to the Good, Dispraise to the Bad; and every one try to mend one’); also SR to Frances Grainger, 29 Mar. 1750 (‘but, I say, every man, every woman, mend one’).
