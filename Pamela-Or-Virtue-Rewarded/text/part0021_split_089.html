Bear-garden: ‘a place in which bears are kept for sport’ and ‘any place of tumult or misrule’ (Johnson). Bear-gardens were used for prize fights as well as bear-baiting (as in Spectator, no. 436, 21 July 1712); their status is indicated in Grandison, where Sir Charles inveighs ‘against the low company into which a dexterity at my weapons might lead me, as well as against the diversions themselves exhibited at the infamous places where those brutal people resorted’ (i. 261).
