my dear Quaker Sister: Mr B. teases her for her antiquated diction and for addressing him as ‘Friend’. The allusion is to the Society of Friends (sect founded by George Fox in 1648–50), whose egalitarian practice of theeing and thouing all ranks is mimicked again in SR’s continuation of 1741, when the pregnant Pamela wears Quaker garb to a masquerade: ‘I hope, Friend, thou art prepar’d with a Father for the Light within thee?’ (iv. 116).
