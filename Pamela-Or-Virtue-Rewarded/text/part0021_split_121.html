In the former case, SR was borrowing a sexually suggestive formula from the novels of Haywood (e.g. ‘trembling … betwixt Desire and Fear, at last she lay resistless in his arms’, in Lasselia (1725), 26–7, which SR had reprinted in 1732). SR quietly changed the phrases in the second edition to read, respectively, ‘betwixt Fear and Joy’ and ‘but that was all’.
