a lively little Boy … born to no Prospect: Harry Campbell (born c.1734; still living 1778), son of John Campbell of Portsmouth, who in adulthood was to become a printer of Popping’s Alley and Carnaby Street. SR promptly rewarded the boy with a gift of illustrated books, including the recent Aesop’s Fables, and in 1751 (after Hill’s death) took him for apprenticeship without the usual fee (Sale, 21; D. F. McKenzie (ed.), Stationers’ Company Apprentices, 1701–1800 (1978), 63, 290).
