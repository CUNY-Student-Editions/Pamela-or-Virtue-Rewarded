the Parental, the Filial, and the Social Duties: i.e. the system of reciprocal obligations, mainly domestic, outlined by William Fleetwood in The Relative Duties of Parents and Children, Husbands and Wives, Masters and Servants (1705), and later extended by Patrick Delany in Fifteen Sermons upon Social Duties (which SR printed in 1744). In Familiar Letters (1741), SR sets out to delineate ‘the social and relative duties’ (Preface); cf. also Clarissa, 135 (‘all the relative … all the social and … all our superior duties’).
