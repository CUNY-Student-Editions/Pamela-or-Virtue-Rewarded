English Bullion in Exchange for its own Dross: the first French translation of Pamela was published in London on 23 October 1741, ‘For the Use of SCHOOLS’ (Daily Gazetteer advertisement of that date). Its prefatory claim to have been produced ‘avec la participation de l’Auteur’ (vol. i, p. x) is borne out by the inclusion of a passage which appeared in no English version of Pamela before the octavo edition of May 1742 (E & K, 125). The translator was probably the author of the present letter, J. B. de Freval (see note below), whom the author of Pamela Censured (1741) guessed to be making a pitch for the job at this point (p. 14). He may have been assisted by others including Caesar de Missy (1703–75), a French chaplain in London, who reportedly translated ‘the four poetical pieces in the French translation of Pamela, 1741’ (J. Nichols, Literary Anecdotes of the Eighteenth Century (1812–15), iii. 307).
