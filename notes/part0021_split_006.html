Your affectionate Friend, &c.: probably the Revd William Webster (1689–1758), editor of the Weekly Miscellany, in which (11 Oct. 1740) this letter had already appeared—though one should not rule out Fielding’s insinuation (Shamela, 309) that SR wrote some of Pamela’s preliminary puffs himself. SR had been printer of the Weekly Miscellany in its early years (1732–6), and had recently forgiven Webster a residual debt of £90(E&K, 58–60).
