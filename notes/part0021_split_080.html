Sack, and a Toast, and Nutmeg, and Sugar: fortified wine from Spain or the Canaries, spiced and sweetened, in which toast would be soaked. Cf. Tatler, no. 24 (4 June 1709): ‘A Toast in a cold Morning, heighten’d by Nutmeg, and sweeten’d with Sugar, has for many Ages been given to our Rural Dispensers of Justice … but has indeed been remarkable for one ill Effect, that it inclines those who use it immoderately to speak Latin’
