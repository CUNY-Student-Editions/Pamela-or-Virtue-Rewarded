officiously: in the neutral, or even approving, sense of dutifully fulfilling her office. Johnson, in 1755, gives ‘kind; doing good offices’ as the primary sense, and makes ‘importunely forward’ secondary. SR uses both senses elsewhere: the second (with hostile application to Mrs Jewkes) above, p. 237; the first, of Pamek (‘I will officiously, with a smiling Countenance, throw myself in their Observation’), in his continuation of 1741 (iv. 265).
